KENYA YOUTH INTER-COUNTY SPORTS ASSOCIATION (KYISA) GAMES 2026
11TH EDITION RULES AND REGULATIONS

Contact: P.O BOX 20655-00200 Nairobi
Email: info.kyisa@gmail.com / ambrosekisoi@gmail.com

Background

The KYISA Games are an annual event designed to unearth and develop raw talent among Kenyan youth. This initiative serves as a vital platform for young individuals who have not yet had the opportunity to excel in mainstream sports pathways.

  • Target: Out-of-school youth aged 18–23 years old, not previously scouted or played above county level.
  • Disciplines: Football, Volleyball, Basketball, Basketball 3x3 (Handball as exhibition).

Eligibility

  • Players must be 18–23 years old (born 2003–2008) with valid Kenyan ID.
  • No players from leagues above County level or institutions (KUSF, Universities, National/Premier/Super/Division I/II, National/International teams).
  • Teams must be county-affiliated and have fulfilled financial obligations.
  • Registration deadline: 1st April 2026.

Payment Details

  • Participation fee: KShs 250,000 per County
  • Pay by 1st April 2026 to:
    Account Name: Kenya Youth Inter-County Sports Association (KYISA)
    Bank: Kenya Commercial Bank
    Account Number: 1241359989
    Branch: Nakuru Main
    IFMS Number: 927986
  • Counties must also cover accommodation, food, transport, medical, and pay athletes KShs 1,000/day during games.

Team Registration

  • Submit detailed team list (Surname, other names, birth date, ID No.) to info.kyisa@gmail.com and via online registration link by 1st April 2026.
  • Carry original National ID for verification at Olkalau Huduma Centre.
  • Director of Sports is responsible for eligibility and authenticity of documents.

Delegation & Referees

  • Football: Max 24 players, 1 FKF Class 2+ referee.
  • Volleyball: Max 14 players, 1 KVF Level 1+ referee.
  • Basketball: Max 12 players, 1 KBF National+ referee.
  • Handball: Max 14 players, 1 KHF National+ referee.
  • Referee CVs/licenses submitted by 1st April 2026, certified by county sports department.

Medical

  • Each county must have at least one medical doctor and provide insurance for participants.

Tournament Format

  • Played under FIFA, FIVB, FIBA rules (with KYISA exceptions).
  • Football: Preliminaries 35-10-35 mins, Knockouts 45-15-45 mins.
  • Volleyball: Best of five sets.
  • Basketball: 4x10 min periods.

Discipline & Appeals

  • Teams must cooperate for safety and successful games.
  • Match abandonment, bad behavior, match fixing, or disruption leads to disqualification and fines.
  • Appeals must be written, submitted within 30 minutes after match, with KShs 2,000 fee (refundable if successful).

Schedule Highlights

  • 17–20 March: Consultative General Meeting
  • 1 April: Confirmation & Payment, Referee CVs, Team List
  • 10–12 April: Referee/Coordinator Arrival, Team Arrival, Managers Meeting
  • 14 April: Player Huduma Verification
  • 15–18 April: Opening Ceremony & Match Days
  • 19 April: Closing Ceremony

Liability Disclaimer

Organizers are not liable for harm/injury. Security and basic first aid will be provided.
